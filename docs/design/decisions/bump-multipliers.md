# Bump Grade Multipliers

**Decision**: All bump grades boost. Only missing the bump entirely is neutral.

## Model

| Grade | Multiplier | Feel |
|-------|-----------|------|
| No bump | 1.0x | Neutral — bolt continues at current speed |
| Early bump | 1.1x | Small boost — you tried, rewarded slightly |
| Late bump | 1.1x | Small boost — same as early |
| Perfect bump | 1.5x | Big boost — the core high-skill payoff |

## Rationale

Punishing attempted bumps contradicts Pillar 3 (Mechanical Floor, Strategic Ceiling). The mechanical floor is "try to bump." If trying and getting an imperfect timing is punished, the floor is "be perfect" — too high.

Rewarding engagement at all grades means:
- Novices feel the game responding to their inputs
- Skilled players chase the perfect window for the big multiplier
- The gap between "trying" and "mastering" is felt, not binary

## Note

Different breakers may use different multiplier profiles (e.g., a risk/reward breaker with higher perfect multiplier but lower early/late). The baseline principle is: attempted bumps always boost.
